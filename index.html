Plain language experiments

It's hard for non-native English readers to understand well-written English. It's hard for a native English reader to get a sense of what that feels like. You can give this a shot and tell me if it helps at all.

I'm going to give you some text that's already hard to read and then give you a weird way to read it. Then you explain what you read. To give you a fair start, I'll tell you it's about housing eviction in Wisconsin.

P.S. These instructions are for coders. I haven't yet made a custom tool that can do this.

  1. Copy the text below. Avoid pasting it anywhere so that the text will be new to you when you read it later.
  2. Go to Sprint Reader.
  3. Scroll down to the demo.
  4. Open the developer tools and, in the console, paste and run this:

    document.querySelector('#spritz').style.transform = 'rotate(180deg)';

  5. Paste the text in your clipboard into the textbox.
  6. Slow down the text to about 40 or so.
  7. Press "Play".
799.29  Default judgments.<\span><\br> (1)  Motion to reopen.<\span><\br> (a) There shall be no appeal from default judgments, but the trial court may, by order, reopen default judgments upon notice and motion or petition duly made and good cause shown.<\span><\br> (b) In ordinance violation cases, the notice of motion must be made within 20 days after entry of judgment. In ordinance violation cases, default judgments for purposes of this section include pleas of guilty, no contest and forfeitures of deposit.<\span><\br> (c) In other actions under this chapter, the notice of motion must be made within 12 months after entry of judgment unless venue was improper under s. 799.11. The court shall order the reopening of a default judgment in an action where venue was improper upon motion or petition duly made within one year after the entry of judgment.<\span><\br>

After you have finished the exercise, you can see the original Wisconsin legal text if you want.
